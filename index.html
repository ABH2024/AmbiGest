AmbiGest: A Dataset of Social Gestures with Inter-Class Similarity and Intra-Class Variability

Accepted to the LFA Workshop at FG 2026

Hajra Anwar Beg, Mohamed Daoudi, Angela Bartolo

Paper (Coming soon) | Dataset (Coming soon) | Code (Coming soon)

AmbiGest is a dataset of 117 dyadic social gesture sequences organized into four communicative categories — greetings, calling, refusal, and thanks — recorded with three actor pairs. It is specifically designed to combine intra-class variability (the same intent performed in different styles) and inter-class similarity (different intents sharing visually similar motion patterns).

To preserve participant anonymity, raw videos are not released. We instead provide 3D SMPL-X representations extracted with two complementary methods, PromptHMR and SMPLest-X, together with hierarchical annotations (class, subclass, semantic group, and natural language description).
